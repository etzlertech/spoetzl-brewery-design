# Design Inspiration Research Swarm

## Purpose

The "Sense" phase is about gathering signals and inspiration that align with
the project's north star. This skill uses a **research agent swarm** to explore
multiple domains simultaneously and synthesize findings into actionable inspiration.

**Key difference from random Pinterest browsing:**
- Filtered through north star alignment
- Multi-domain perspective (history, modern, climate, materials)
- Synthesized into actionable design principles
- Documented for team reference

## Research Swarm Composition

Four specialized research agents explore different domains in parallel:

### Agent 1: Historical Design Researcher (Explore)
**Expertise**: Traditional design patterns and cultural heritage
**Focus**: Authentic historical precedents for German brewery/beer garden design

**Research Domains**:
- Traditional German beer garden layouts
- European village landscaping patterns
- German horticultural traditions
- Historical materials and construction methods

**Output**: Historical design patterns, cultural authenticity guidelines

### Agent 2: Modern Examples Researcher (Explore)
**Expertise**: Contemporary successful implementations
**Focus**: Modern beer gardens, brewery landscapes in US

**Research Domains**:
- Contemporary US breweries with German theming
- Busch Gardens Williamsburg design elements
- Modern beer garden installations (Austin, Denver, Portland)
- Successful theme park landscaping

**Output**: Case studies, modern materials, proven approaches

### Agent 3: Climate Adaptation Specialist (Explore)
**Expertise**: Texas-appropriate plant selections and materials
**Focus**: What thrives in Texas heat while maintaining European aesthetic

**Research Domains**:
- Texas-hardy plants with European character
- Heat-tolerant landscaping materials
- Irrigation strategies for Texas climate
- Seasonal interest in Zone 8b

**Output**: Curated plant lists, material recommendations, climate strategies

### Agent 4: Materials & Techniques Scout (Explore)
**Expertise**: Hardscape materials, construction techniques
**Focus**: Authentic-looking materials available locally

**Research Domains**:
- Limestone and natural stone options
- Decomposed granite vs. pea gravel
- Timber construction (pergolas, trellises)
- Metal fabrication (gates, railings)

**Output**: Material sourcing guide, technique recommendations

## Research Process

```markdown
STEP 1: Define Research Parameters
- Project: Spoetzl Brewery landscape enhancement
- Vision: German heritage + European village + guest experience
- Constraints: Texas climate, budget, maintenance capacity

STEP 2: Spawn Research Swarm (Parallel Agents)
- Launch all 4 research agents simultaneously
- Each explores their specialized domain
- Each agent filters findings through north star alignment

STEP 3: Collect Research Findings
- Historical: Traditional design principles and authentic elements
- Modern: Successful contemporary examples and innovations
- Climate: Texas-appropriate selections and strategies
- Materials: Available options and sourcing information

STEP 4: Synthesis Agent (General-Purpose)
- Receives all research outputs
- Identifies common themes across domains
- Highlights contradictions or tradeoffs
- Generates actionable inspiration guide

STEP 5: Generate Inspiration Document
- Key design principles extracted from research
- Curated image references (descriptions for sourcing)
- Plant and material recommendations
- Design pattern catalog
- "Avoid these" warnings (inauthentic, climate-inappropriate)
```

## Example: Beer Garden Seating Area Research

**User Request**: "Research inspiration for outdoor seating area with German beer garden feel"

**Research Swarm Execution**:

**Agent 1 (Historical)**:
- Findings: Traditional beer gardens use long communal tables (6-8' long)
- Materials: Heavy timber tables, simple benches (no backs)
- Layout: Tables in gravel courtyards under shade trees
- Social pattern: Communal seating encourages conversation

**Agent 2 (Modern)**:
- Case Study: Scholz Garten (Austin, TX) - oldest beer garden in Texas
- Success: Mix of communal tables + individual tables for flexibility
- Innovation: String lights overhead create ambiance
- Lesson: Americans prefer some individual table options

**Agent 3 (Climate)**:
- Shade is CRITICAL in Texas - need pergola, umbrellas, or mature trees
- Gravel surface stays cooler than concrete
- Fans are essential for summer comfort
- Consider winter: fire pits or heaters extend season

**Agent 4 (Materials)**:
- Local cedar for table construction (durable, cost-effective)
- Decomposed granite vs. pea gravel for surface
- Metal pergola frames available from local fabricators
- String light options: Edison bulbs vs. cafe lights

**Synthesis**:
- Design principle: "Communal with flexibility"
- Implementation: 60% communal tables, 40% 4-person tables
- Materials: Cedar tables, decomposed granite, metal pergola
- Climate adaptation: Full shade coverage, ceiling fans, optional fire pits
- Authentic + practical balance achieved


## Success Criteria

- Research is filtered through project vision (not random ideas)
- Multiple perspectives prevent single-track thinking
- Climate reality prevents beautiful-but-doomed designs
- Material sourcing is practical and local
- Design principles are actionable, not just aesthetic
- Team has shared reference vocabulary

## Integration with Other Skills

- Start of every major design element (Sense phase)
- Check alignment with `/north-star-check`
- Feed into `/design-spec-review` for specification
- Validate feasibility with `/landscape-scoping-lessons`
- Generate visuals with `/clarity-engine`

## Notes

- Research agents use Explore subagent_type for fast information gathering
- Synthesis agent uses general-purpose for complex reasoning
- All research filtered through vision alignment
- No external API calls - pure Claude Code agent orchestration
