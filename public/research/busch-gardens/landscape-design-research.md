# Busch Gardens Williamsburg Landscape Design Research

## Executive Summary

Busch Gardens Williamsburg has been recognized as the "World's Most Beautiful Theme Park" for 29 consecutive years by the National Amusement Park Historical Association (NAPHA) and has received the Golden Ticket Award for Best Landscaping for 22 consecutive years from Amusement Today. The park's landscape design philosophy centers on creating intensely compelling visual experiences that enhance European theming while preserving and integrating natural terrain features.

---

## 1. Overall Landscape Design Approach and Philosophy

### Design Philosophy

**Intensity and Impact**: According to landscape manager Eileen Weldon, the landscape must be compelling to compete with roller coasters and street performers. The design philosophy emphasizes intensive, eye-catching plantings that enhance the guest experience rather than simply providing background decoration.

**Natural Terrain Preservation**: When originally built in 1975, care was taken to preserve the mature landscaping and natural terrain of the park. Some trees on site are over 400 years old, demonstrating the park's commitment to preserving existing natural assets.

**Creating "Wow Moments"**: The park is designed to take advantage of natural topography to create surprise moments for visitors, such as walking along a landscaped pathway and having a roller coaster train rush by overhead.

**Authenticity and Detail**: The park is committed to authentic and accurate theming, carefully depicting each detail from quaint cobblestone streets and building facades to designer landscapes and beautiful statuary.

### Scale of Operations

- **Park Size**: Approximately 390 acres total, with 117 acres of intensively landscaped areas
- **Turf Management**: About 70 acres of turf
- **Landscape Team**: 22 full-time and 6 part-time members
- **Flower Installations**:
  - 900 containers
  - 250 flower beds
  - 150 hanging baskets
  - Total plantings exceed 20,000 spring annuals plus 60,000 summer annuals

### Maintenance Schedule

- **Daily Operations**: Grooming, watering, and plant replacement occurs daily prior to park opening
- **Early Morning Crews**: 6 a.m. watering crews deploy across the property with 100-gallon tanks
- **Seasonal Rotations**: Floral displays are replaced approximately six times yearly
- **Spring Preparation**: 4,800 cubic yards of mulch spread before seasonal openings
- **Sod Installation**: Approximately 1,200 square yards of sod installed seasonally
- **Additional Materials**: 2,250 square yards of mulch and 300 square yards of wood chips applied in spring
- **Tree Pruning**: Over 650 crape myrtles pruned annually

## 2. European Theme Integration into Landscape Architecture

### Themed Village Hamlets

The park features 10 different hamlets themed to traditional interpretations of European villages:
- **England**
- **Scotland**
- **Ireland**
- **France**
- **Germany**
- **Italy**

### Historical Period Selection

The park avoided any thematic connection to the colonial period to prevent conflict with Colonial Williamsburg, instead choosing a pre-colonial, European theme set around the 16th century.

### Landscape Integration with Theming

**France**: Parterre beds (formal geometric garden beds) reflect French formal garden traditions

**Germany**: Alpine scents and plantings appropriate to German mountainous regions

**Ride-Specific Theming**: Flower gardens vary in size and floral composition to accentuate the themes of nearby rides or venues. For example, dark witchhazel shrubs were planted to enhance the DarKastle ride atmosphere.

### Authentic Design Elements

- Quaint cobblestone streets
- Country-specific architecture
- Beautiful statuary
- Lush landscaping throughout
- Themed shops and cafes integrated into village settings

## 3. Horticulture and Plant Selection Strategies

### Spring Plantings (Pre-Opening to Early May)

**Annual Varieties** (20,000+ plants):
- Violas
- Pansies
- Dusty miller
- Calibrachoas
- Snap dragons
- Dianthus
- Petunias
- Agryanthemums
- Cyclamens
- Osteos
- Euphorbias
- Geraniums
- Lobelias
- Green and variegated ivies

**Bulb Plantings**: 14,000-50,000 flowering bulbs (primarily tulips) planted during winter preparation

**Selection Criteria**: Hardy varieties that can withstand potential late-winter weather conditions

### Summer Plantings (Early May through Fall)

**Annual and Tropical Varieties** (60,000+ plants):
- Hibiscus
- Salvias
- Begonias
- Marigolds
- Lantana
- Petunias
- Geraniums

**Selection Criteria**: Heat-resistant varieties capable of withstanding Virginia's hot, humid summers

### Fall Plantings

**Primary Focus**: Chrysanthemums in various colors and varieties

### In-House Production

- **Greenhouse Facility**: 17,000 square feet
- **Annual Production**: 30,000 plants produced in-house
- **Sustainable Pest Management**: Beneficial insects (ladybugs, predatory wasps, lacewings) introduced over 10 years ago, eliminating chemical pesticides in greenhouse production

## 4. Topography Usage and Natural Terrain Integration

### Natural Terrain Features

**Elevation**: The park's approximate elevation is 49 feet (15 meters) above sea level, with significant variation throughout the property

**Topographical Character**: The park is described as "pretty hilly," with natural ravines, valleys, and elevation changes

**Central Water Feature**: The park is centered on a river-like lake in a canyon, with themed areas laid out in a loop path surrounding it

### Terrain-Integrated Attractions

**Loch Ness Monster**:
- Described as a "terrain coaster" specifically built to follow the area's natural contours
- Drops 114 feet into a natural ravine
- First major attraction to take full advantage of the park's natural scenery and terrain

**Apollo's Chariot**:
- Features a 170-foot lift with a much larger drop into a water-filled ravine
- Utilizes natural elevation changes for dramatic effect

**InvadR**:
- 2,118-foot track carefully integrated into the existing terrain of the New France section
- Utilizes natural ravines and wooded areas
- Achieves compact footprint while preserving existing landscape

**Alpengeist**:
- Flies under bridges and hugs ravines on the second half of the ride
- Integrates seamlessly with terrain and landscape

**Rhine River Valley**:
- Multiple coasters concentrated at the bottom of the Rhine River valley
- Creates exceptional viewing opportunities where landscape and attractions intersect
- The Rhine River Cruise is located at the base of a steep hill, highlighting significant elevation changes

### Design Integration Philosophy

Every coaster integrates with the terrain, landscaping, and layout of the park, utilizing natural features rather than fighting against them. This creates a cohesive experience where attractions enhance rather than dominate the natural beauty.

## 5. Water Features and Their Role in the Design

### Major Water Features

**Central Lake**:
- River-like lake in a canyon serves as the park's centerpiece
- Themed areas arranged in a loop surrounding the water feature

**Rhine River Cruise**:
- Battery-powered boats reduce power generation and prevent water pollution
- Showcases the park's beautiful landscapes
- Passes beneath Loch Ness Monster coaster, creating dramatic viewing opportunities

**Water-Filled Ravines**:
- Natural ravines enhanced with water features
- Integrated with coaster experiences (e.g., Apollo's Chariot drop)

**Interactive Water Elements**:
- One area features over 100 interactive water elements
- Includes cannons, sprayers, and tipping fountains

**Maintained Water Features**:
- 5 major water features requiring regular landscape team maintenance

### Environmental Considerations

The Rhine River Cruise uses battery-powered boats specifically to cut back on power generation and prevent water pollution, demonstrating environmental stewardship in water feature management.

## 6. Seasonal Plantings and Color Programs

### Seasonal Rotation Schedule

The park maintains approximately six complete floral rotations per year, ensuring peak appearance throughout all operating seasons.

### Spring Program

**Timing**: Winter preparation through early May

**Bulb Display**:
- 14,000-50,000 tulips providing massive spring color impact
- Planted during winter months for spring emergence

**Annual Display**:
- 20,000+ cold-hardy annuals in containers, beds, and hanging baskets
- Emphasis on early-blooming varieties and cool-season tolerance

**Color Palette**:
- Purples and yellows (pansies, violas)
- Whites and pastels (dusty miller, cyclamens)
- Mixed colors (dianthus, petunias, geraniums)

### Summer Program

**Timing**: Early May through fall

**Annual Display**:
- 60,000+ heat-resistant annuals and tropical plants
- Largest seasonal installation due to extended operating season

**Color Palette**:
- Bold, vibrant colors (hibiscus, salvias)
- Warm tones (begonias, marigolds)
- Mixed tropicals (lantana)

**Design Strategy**:
- Intense color to maintain visual impact during peak visitation
- Heat-tolerant selections to ensure sustained performance

### Fall Program

**Primary Focus**:
- Chrysanthemums in traditional fall colors
- Transition plantings to extend season

### Winter/Holiday Program

**Christmas Town**:
- Seasonal decorations and lighting
- Live Christmas trees used during holiday season
- Trees recycled into wood chips for spring landscaping (sustainability practice)

### Design Considerations

**Thematic Alignment**:
- Color programs and plant selections aligned with European village themes
- Formal plantings in French areas
- Alpine-appropriate selections in German areas

**Ride Integration**:
- Plantings selected to enhance specific attraction theming
- Dark foliage for dramatic rides
- Bright colors for family-friendly areas

## 7. Sustainability Practices in Landscape Maintenance

### Integrated Pest Management

**Beneficial Insect Program**:
- Introduced over 10 years ago in greenhouse production
- Ladybugs, predatory wasps, and lacewings replace chemical pesticides
- Successfully eliminates need for chemical pest control in production facilities

**Organic Commitment**:
- Both Busch Gardens Williamsburg and Water Country USA use insects rather than pesticides
- Park-wide commitment to organic gardening practices

### Resource Conservation

**Water Management**:
- Rhine River Cruise uses battery-powered boats to reduce power generation and prevent water pollution
- Strategic watering schedules (6 a.m. crews with 100-gallon tanks)

**Material Recycling**:
- Christmas trees from holiday season recycled into wood chips for spring landscaping
- Demonstrates closed-loop thinking in material management

### Environmental Certification

**Native Plant Habitats**:
- Certified native plant habitat around the Lorikeet aviary
- Provides educational opportunity while supporting local ecosystems

### Conservation Partnership

**SeaWorld Partnership**:
- Conservation funding collaboration for animal rescue and habitat protection
- Extends sustainability mission beyond park boundaries

### Long-Term Resource Stewardship

**Mature Tree Preservation**:
- Original 1975 park development preserved existing mature trees
- Some specimens over 400 years old maintained and protected
- Demonstrates long-term commitment to natural resource conservation

**Sustainable Operations**:
- In-house greenhouse production reduces transportation impacts
- Local plant production supports regional horticulture industry

## Recognition and Awards

### Industry Recognition

- **World's Most Beautiful Theme Park**: 29 consecutive years (1990-2019+) - National Amusement Park Historical Association (NAPHA)
- **Golden Ticket Award for Best Landscaping**: 22 consecutive years - Amusement Today

### Competitive Distinction

Busch Gardens Williamsburg's immersive theming, attention to landscaping, and cultural influences set it apart from other U.S. theme parks. The park is nestled among Virginia's forests and rivers, creating a unique setting that enhances rather than replaces the natural environment.

## Key Takeaways for Landscape Design

1. **Intensity Matters**: In high-stimulation environments, landscapes must be compelling and intensive to achieve impact

2. **Preserve Natural Assets**: Working with existing topography and mature plantings creates authenticity and reduces long-term maintenance

3. **Seasonal Rotation**: Multiple complete changeovers per year maintain peak appearance and guest experience

4. **Theme Integration**: Landscape selections should reinforce and enhance overall design themes

5. **Sustainability as Standard**: Beneficial insects, material recycling, and native plantings can be integrated without compromising aesthetic quality

6. **Scale Planning**: Large teams and substantial material investments are required for consistent excellence at scale

7. **Early Intervention**: Daily morning maintenance prevents problems and maintains standards

8. **In-House Production**: Greenhouse facilities provide control, consistency, and cost management

9. **Topography as Asset**: Natural terrain features provide opportunities for dramatic experiences when integrated thoughtfully

10. **Recognition Reflects Commitment**: Consistent industry awards demonstrate that landscape excellence requires sustained long-term investment
