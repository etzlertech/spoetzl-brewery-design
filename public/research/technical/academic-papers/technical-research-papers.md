## Academic Research on Theme Park Landscape Architecture

### 1. "Theme Park Landscapes: Antecedents and Variations" (2002)

**Citation:**
Young, Terence G., and Robert B. Riley (Eds.). *Theme Park Landscapes: Antecedents and Variations*. Washington, D.C.: Dumbarton Oaks Research Library and Collection, 2002. 300 pp.

**ISBN:** 0-88402-285-4

**Publisher:** Dumbarton Oaks Research Library and Collection (Dumbarton Oaks Colloquium on the History of Landscape Architecture XX)

**Abstract/Summary:**
This seminal work examines the historical and contemporary development of theme park landscapes, addressing how theme parks became a metaphor for postmodern urban life during the 1990s. The book's twelve essays examine current and past, public and private, obviously and subtly themed landscapes across Asia, Europe, and North America.

**Key Technical Findings:**
- **Historical Precedents:** Sixteenth- to nineteenth-century European gardens were the major progenitors of theme parks, with landscape designers such as André Le Nôtre and William Kent transforming spaces rich with visual references
- **"Disneyfication" Concept:** Analysis of how themed landscapes communicate with multiple audiences and stimulate consumption
- **Scholarly Gap:** Few scholarly studies focus specifically on landscapes in theme parks, making this volume particularly valuable
- **Geographic Scope:** Essays cover diverse locations including Asia, Europe, and North America

**Symposium Origin:**
Essays developed from the May 1996 Studies in Landscape Architecture Symposium organized by Joachim Wolschke-Bulmahn and Robert B. Riley

**Availability:**
- Full text available at [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/young-riley-theme-park-landscapes)
- Purchase: [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Theme-Park-Landscapes-Antecedents-Architecture/dp/0884022854)
- [Dumbarton Oaks Publication](https://www.doaks.org/resources/publications/books/theme-park-landscapes-antecedents-and-variations)

**Relevance to Busch Gardens Williamsburg:**
Provides historical context for European-themed landscape design in North American theme parks, directly applicable to BGW's design philosophy.

  - Facilities and rides

## Visitor Experience & Landscape Design Correlation

### 1. "What keeps historical theme park visitors coming? Research based on expectation confirmation theory"

**Citation:**
Published in *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2024

**DOI:** Available at [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1293638/full)

**Also Available:** [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10937583/)

**Research Focus:**
- Factors affecting tourist travel experience in historical theme parks
- Formation of tourist satisfaction
- Post-trip behavioral intentions
- Expectation confirmation theory application

**Key Findings:**
- **Thematic Characteristics:** A great majority of the most visited theme parks have thematic and narrative characteristics that provide appeal to visitors
- **Quality Experience Impact:** If travelers' overall sense of travel experience is of quality, it positively impacts their satisfaction
- **Authentic Experiences:** Rich cultural backgrounds and authentic environmental experiences increase trip value and make travel experiences more meaningful

**Relevance to Busch Gardens Williamsburg:**
Demonstrates measurable correlation between thematic landscape quality and visitor satisfaction/return visits - directly applicable to BGW's European theming strategy.

### 2. Disney and Universal Immersive Design Research

**Disney Landscape Architecture Principles:**

**Immersive Environment Creation:**
- Focus on storytelling within outdoor environments
- Natural extension of narrative themes
- Integration of site programming with entertainment needs

**Leadership Role:**
Landscape Architects at Disney integrate:
- Site programming
- Functional requirements
- Operational needs
- Entertainment elements
- Aesthetic visions

**Key Ingredients of Immersive Landscape Design:**
1. **Visual Containment:** Landscaped berms to block external visual intrusions
2. **Material Palette:** Paving, walls, fences coordinated to reinforce character
3. **Water Features:** Design details supporting thematic narrative
4. **Color and Materials:** Careful selection to evoke specific emotions
5. **Tree Canopy:** Strategic placement to manage views and create atmosphere
6. **Forced Perspective:** Architectural and landscape techniques to enhance immersion
7. **Berms:** Isolation from outside world to maintain thematic integrity

**Design Philosophy:**
"Disney architecture isn't merely about creating aesthetically pleasing buildings; it's about telling a story, where each building and detail contributes to the larger narrative of the park, with design choices from color palettes to materials carefully considered to evoke specific emotions and immerse guests in the intended atmosphere."

### 3. Beneficial Insect Research

**Ladybugs (Lady Beetles):**
- Well-known, widely used in greenhouse pest control
- Primary prey: aphids, scale insects, mealybugs, mites
- Highly effective aphid population reduction

**Green Lacewings:**
- Voracious predators of soft-bodied pests
- Target pests: aphids, thrips, whiteflies, caterpillar eggs
- Larvae particularly beneficial
- Active prey searching behavior
- High feeding rates during development

**Parasitoid Wasps:**
- Small, non-stinging species
- Lay eggs inside host pests
- Larvae feed on host, causing death
- Species-specific targeting

### 5. IPM and Insecticide Reduction

**Central Concern:**
- Reduction of insecticide use is crucial for enhancing biological control
- Natural enemies highly susceptible to insecticides
- Inverse relationship: as insecticide use increases, beneficial insect populations decrease

**Research Finding:**
"Reduction of insecticide use is a central concern of most IPM programs and is crucial for enhancing biological control, as natural enemies can be highly susceptible to insecticides, and as insecticide use increases, populations of beneficial insects decrease."

- 2006-2008: Busch Gardens Europe (briefly)

**Colonial Avoidance:**
- Land use stipulation: could not use colonial American theme
- Avoided conflict with nearby Colonial Williamsburg historical sites
- Pre-colonial European theme chosen (circa 16th century)

**European Village Theming:**
- 10 different hamlets representing traditional European villages
- Countries represented:
  - England
  - France
  - Germany
  - Italy
  - Scotland
  - Ireland

**Each Section Includes:**

## Conclusion

This technical research compilation reveals that while direct academic research specifically on Busch Gardens Williamsburg is limited, the park serves as a premier example of landscape excellence in the theme park industry. The 35-year consecutive recognition as "World's Most Beautiful Park" by NAPHA, combined with 22 consecutive Golden Ticket Awards for landscaping, demonstrates sustained excellence unmatched in the industry.

The research compiled here provides a foundation for understanding:
- Theme park landscape design principles and methodologies
- Large-scale sustainable horticulture practices
- Visitor experience correlation with landscape quality
- Integrated pest management using beneficial insects
- European garden design adaptation to North American climates
- Sustainable water feature technologies
- Thematic immersive environment creation

**Opportunity for Future Research:**
Busch Gardens Williamsburg represents an exceptional opportunity for comprehensive academic case study research using established landscape architecture methodologies. Such research would fill significant gaps in the literature and provide valuable insights for the theme park industry, landscape architecture profession, and horticulture field.
