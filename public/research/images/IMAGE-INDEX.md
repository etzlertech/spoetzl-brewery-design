## Design Principles Cross-Reference

### Principle 1: Intensity and Impact
**Images:** 01, 02, 06, 07, 09, 10, 24
- Background plantings insufficient in high-stimulation environments
- Bold colors, massive displays, eye-catching compositions
- Seasonal rotations maintain peak appearance

### Principle 2: Natural Asset Preservation
**Images:** 03, 04, 14, 15, 23
- Work with existing topography and terrain
- Terrain-integrated coasters, water features enhancing natural features
- Elevation changes create dramatic reveals

### Principle 3: Multi-Sensory Integration
**Images:** 04, 05, 08, 11, 16
- Visual (architecture), auditory (water), tactile (paving materials)
- European architectural details
- Varied textures and materials

### Principle 4: Thematic Authenticity
**Images:** 05, 11, 12, 13, 20, 21, 22
- Balance historical accuracy with theatrical interpretation
- Country-specific architecture (German, Italian, French, Scottish, English)
- Period-appropriate design elements

### Principle 5: Seasonal Variation
**Images:** 06, 07, 09, 10
- Multiple rotations maintain freshness (6 per year)
- Spring bulbs (50,000 tulips), summer annuals (60,000 plants)
- Christmas Town overlay (10M+ lights)

### Principle 6: Water as Organizing Element
**Images:** 04, 14, 16, 23
- Central water features create focal points
- Bridges mark transitions
- Battery-powered sustainability

### Principle 7: Integration Strategies
**Images:** 03, 08, 15, 19
- Visual, narrative, acoustic, functional integration
- Attractions integrated with landscape
- Circulation and wayfinding design

### Principle 8: Operational Excellence
**Images:** 20, 24
- In-house production (30,000 plants annually)
- Precision maintenance (22 full-time professionals)
- Daily intervention and quality standards
